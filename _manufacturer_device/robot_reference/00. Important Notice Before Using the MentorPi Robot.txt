     Important Notice Before Using the
             MentorPi Robot
The MentorPi robot is based on ROS and involves multiple modules and
relatively complex operations. To ensure you have the best experience using
this product, we strongly recommend reading this document in advance.


This guide is intended for users with a basic background in electronics,
information technology, and computer science. Familiarity with Linux, Python,
and ROS is recommended.


To support your learning, we have included several foundational tutorials in the
resource package. You can find them in the “1 Tutorial Materials” folder,

1. Important Notes Before Use

1.1 Battery Charging

Caution:
1.   Do not connect the battery lead wires directly to the lithium battery before connecting

     it to the controller. Direct contact of the positive and negative terminals may cause a

     short circuit.

2.   To ensure stable operation of the robot, please charge the battery promptly when the

     voltage drops below 6.4V. Use only the charger provided in the kit for charging.


Due to transportation regulations, lithium batteries are not fully charged when
shipped. Before first use, please fully charge the battery. Users should first
connect the battery to the lead wires, then proceed with charging. Charging
typically takes around 1 hour.

Charging Steps:

1) Make sure the device is turned off.

2) Match the battery wires by color: connect red to red and black to black.

1.3 System Configuration After Power-On

The MentorPi robot supports different chassis types (Mecanum wheel,
Ackermann) and camera versions. If you need to switch between different
robot configurations or camera models, please follow the steps below:


Note:


   If the chassis type is incorrectly configured, the robot may not respond to motion

    commands properly, or its movement trajectory and direction may behave

    abnormally.

   If the wrong camera type is selected, image data may not appear on the mobile app

    or Raspberry Pi desktop interface.


In such cases, please refer to the following instructions to reconfigure the system.


1) Power on the robot and connect it to the remote control software VNC.
    For guidance on connecting via remote desktop, refer to: 8. Set
    Development Environment\ Lesson 1 VNC Installation and
    Connection




2) On the remote desktop, double-click the                      “Tool” icon to launch
    the system configuration utility, then click the “Execute” button.




3) In the interface, under Camera Type, select the appropriate camera
   version:




     1   ascamera: Orbbec depth camera
     2   usb_cam: Standard monocular camera with 2D pan-tilt


4) Under Robot Type, select the corresponding chassis:




     1   MentorPi_Mecanum: Mecanum wheel chassis
     2   MentorPi_Acker: Ackermann chassis


5) After making your selections, click Save → Apply → Exit in order. It’s
    crucial to follow this sequence to ensure the settings take effect correctly.




6) Wait for the buzzer to emit a short beep, indicating that the chassis type
    has been successfully switched. You can open the terminal to check the

    ROS 2 environment configuration           .

7) Once the configuration is complete, proceed to: 1. Getting Ready\ 1.4 App
    Control; 1.5 Wireless Controller Control.
